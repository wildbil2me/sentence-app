# Code of Conduct

Grammar Lab is a small classroom tool built and used mostly by teachers. The
rules follow from that.

## The short version

- **Assume good faith.** Most disagreements here are about how grammar is taught,
  and reasonable teachers disagree. "My textbook calls it a predicate nominative"
  is useful information, not an attack.
- **Be useful to the next teacher.** Explain *why* a label or a workflow matters
  in a classroom. Context is what makes a report actionable.
- **No harassment, personal attacks, demeaning comments, or unwelcome attention** —
  in issues, pull requests, discussions, or commit messages.
- **Keep student data out of the repository.** Never paste student names, work, or
  grades into an issue. Grammar Lab stores nothing on a server; keep it that way
  in the issue tracker too. See [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).
- **Passages should be classroom-appropriate.** Example lessons and bug reports
  get read by other people's students.

## Scope

This applies to everything in this repository and to anywhere someone is
representing the project.

## Enforcement

Report a problem to the maintainer at **wildbil2me@gmail.com**. Reports are
handled privately. The maintainer may edit or delete comments, close issues, or
block accounts, at their discretion and without a debate about it.
